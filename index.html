About Me

I am a recent grad with a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics with diligent training in software development and engineering, object oriented design, and pure mathematics.

I fluently speak English, Spanish, and French, which has given me the ability to conceptualize, and explain complex ideas both in written and verbal platforms. This disposition for languages has also provided me with an acute attention to details for context, structure, and clear communication.  

A topic that is really important to me is diversity in the STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields.  During my senior year, I volunteered for Girls Investigate Science and Math Opportunities (GISMO) sharing my passion for Computer Science with middle school girls. I also worked to open a chapter of SUKUMA, a supportive community for underrepresented students in  computer science, engineering, physics, and mathematics in my university. The goal of this group is to retain these students in their academic departments using a series of proven strategies used by companies like Google and Project Include. We provided these resources and support to 40 at-risk minority students who all decided to continue in their STEM fields thanks to SUKUMA.

My senior thesis studied and compared the strategies used to retain minority groups in STEM fields. You can request the published version of the thesis here, or by emailing me directly.

In my free time I like to practice circus arts (aerial hoop and trapeze are my favorites) and I practice fire spinning. I am also an avid environmentalist and an active vegetarian.
